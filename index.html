Crop residue burning is a major contributor to air pollution in many agricultural regions. Over the past decades, there has been a significant increase in the incidence of stubble burning after harvests, especially of rice and wheat. This releases large amounts of particulate matter, carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides and other pollutants into the air, leading to severe deterioration of air quality.

The situation is very adverse in India due to the rice-wheat rotation system which generates large amount of stubble. The haze of smog observed over India during the winter season is due to stubble burning since it coincides with the burning periods (October-November). During this time, Indian cities like Delhi experience harsh pollution often reaching the severe levels of the air quality index.
